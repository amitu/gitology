Read about the Nokia-Microsoft canoodle on v-day, and went running to check what Russ has to say about it. Some context information to appreciate the real significance of this story is here, as I talked about earlier. Microsoft is putting pieces of its plan together, but I think something else might be going on here too. They recently announced support for Python on their devices, and then brought Macromedia Flash into the picture. They also silently developed a Nokia Browser and licensed ActiveSync from Microsoft, for emails and stuff. Russ says Nokia-MS deal is a precursor to a Nokia branded Windows Mobile phone but if you put it all together, it seems on contrary. Nokia seems to have no plans to switch to Windows Mobile, [though they would probably roll out a few models with Windows Mobile to meet the demands of higher end users], rather they focusing on their own platform(Symbian) and are putting great efforts in enhancing it. If this is so we may also expect some licensing deals of Nokia with Apple for their iTunes service [this would blow a hole in Motorola's hopes of playing an important role in possible Microsoft-Nokia vs Apple-Motorola checkmate scenario if Nokia ignored Apple].
