So you have read enough RFCs, and thought they were boring, were they RFC-1438-IETF-Statements-of-Boredom boring? Or, oh you think you can parse any mime compliant email message, well can you parse "electronic mail messages that include entire sentient life forms, such as human beings" as attachments? And is your coffee pot Hyper Text Coffee Pot Control Protocol (HTCPCP/1.0) compliant? This one is my fav: the IPng focused on choosing an addressing scheme that makes it possible to talk to everyone while dispensing with the irrelevant overhead of actually having to say anything. Another gem: enough of voice over IP, can you transmit electricity over IP? exactly what Reliance should be looking at. :-)


All these and more at Marcel Waldvogel: Fun with RFCs. They have poems too!


PS: Request for Comment(RFC) are the draft that goes on and becomes internet standards like HTTP and Internet Email.
