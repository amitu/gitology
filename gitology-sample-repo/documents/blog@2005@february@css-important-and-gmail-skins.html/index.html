Came across skins for GMail on GMailTools blog. Why would one want to do that? For many "because they can" is good enough reason, plus it also lets you control a few things [like hiding the invite a friend thing or the footer as demonstrated in screenshot].

Anyways, there I discovered !important override in CSS. Suddenly I realised I can use it to make my CSS popup hackThis is a pure CSS popup, visible only if you hover the mouse over it. It can also include images. look a little less ugly in bloglines. The idea is to replace a:hover span {display: block; by a:hover span {display: block !important;. And adding a style="display: none;" in the inner span. Example: <a href="...">Sometext<span style="display: none;">Hidden text</span></a>
