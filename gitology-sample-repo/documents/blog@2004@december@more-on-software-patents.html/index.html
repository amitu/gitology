arstechnica has a story about the software patent mess:
Now that we've got a few years' distance from the USPTO's decision to start granting patents on software, we can step back and take stock of some of the results. In short, a slow-motion train wreck is unfolding in the industry. As the number of software patents zooms past the 100,000 mark, the number of accidental infringers has grown to include almost anyone who writes a decent-sized application, and the amount of money spent on lawyering has gone through the roof. The only clear winners are the lawyers; the clear losers are everyone else.
