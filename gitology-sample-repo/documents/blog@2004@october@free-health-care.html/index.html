free healthcare frightens me...

...as a libertarian. Because "free" actually means "paid for by taxes", which means the individual has less control over the cost (no competition) and quality of care received (doctors don't have to compete).

Here is the summary of the debate that follows.

anathema gives the example of Canada, which has free health care for all:

I think the best and worst thing about canadian health professionals is they are immune to civil lawsuits.

  • This is great because they dont need malpractice insurance..keeps costs much lower.

  • This is terrible because while they are technically answerable to peer review, they are still much more lax and less paranoid in treating/diagnosing patients.

There are people who die from terminal diseases because the doctor only diagnoses a cold or some such. My gf's dad has crohn's disease, and took him forever to get a doctor to run tests because of his severe abdominal pain. They let it go for so long that he had to have a piece of his intestine removed. A doctor in the states would be willing to do any test the patient would pay for..not jsut tell him to avoid certain foots, etc.

The debate is at two levels, one about the perils and benefits of the medical lawsuits, highly prevalent in US. Keeps them at toes vs keeps them costly. At the other end the debate is about "the Libertarians believe that the free market can fix any and all problems that could possibly come up" as NightHwk1 points out.

A conservative, but rather lame rebuttal of "low quality of care received (doctors don't have to compete)" argument:

People who become doctors rarely do it for the money. They typically do it for the love of people and the love of the job. Personally, I prefer it that way -- I'd rather be operated on by someone who wants to be in that profession rather than by someone who is just there for the money, and who will cut whatever corners they can to increase their productivity and bottom line. Wouldn't you?
