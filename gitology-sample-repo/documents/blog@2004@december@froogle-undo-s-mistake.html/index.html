As I mentioned before, Froogle, Google's shopping search service, has started a wishlist feature recently, through which you can communicate your loved ones what is that you want as gift this christmas or birthday. Amazon has a similer feature, but since I last checked they don't give you a URL to share on your blog etc (you can only email it to people, a bit blunt I suppose). Anyways, I had problems with Froogle using email address in the URL that they generate to share, invariably that will land up on homepages/blogs, as that is their sole/main purpose, and ultimately to spammers. But thankfully they have rectified this mistake, and now use a wierd hexadecimal id like this.

BTW in case you are in a situation you have to put your email address on a webpage, consider atleast encoding it.
