Google Labs has unveiled a new service, Google Suggest:
Today we launched Google Suggest, a new Labs project that provides you with search suggestions, in real time, while you type. We've found that Google Suggest not only makes it easier to type in your favorite searches (let's face it -- we're all a little lazy), but also gives you a playground to explore what others are searching about, and learn about things you haven't dreamt of. Go ahead, give it a spin.

Other than the fact that its quite innovative and blazingly fast, (from the same announcement) one may notice:
What's really amazed me about this project is how in a matter of months, working on my own, I was able to go from a lunch table conversation to launching a new service.

What I am trying to emphasise here is not that he is too cool or Google totally allows/encourages such stuff(skunk work) and thus is really great company, both are true nevertheless, rather the fact that it was so easy. Infact this is not even new, there is an open source implementation (LiveSearch) since atleast July this year (demo). The basic technology behind them is DHTML and WebServices and numerous books are available on both topics on any bookstore since years, and yet these are somehow "cutting edge". GMail and Flickr extensively uses them, and are the coolest services in their respective field, yet ironically so many technology companies shy away from technology.
