Dot, KDE's news site, is running a story about a port of KHTML to GTK, the "rival" GUI library. The port is called Gtk WebCore and is contributed by Nokia Research Center. KHTML was also picked by Apple, who contributed a lot of code to it, for their Safari browser, and webcore programming framework.

So why is Nokia, a cell phone company, interested in a browser? It may have something to do with the Konqueror Embedded. It already runs on embedded devices, but porting it to GTK will put it in the LGPL domain, and avoid the "viral nature of GPL"[KHTML itself is LGPL], or they already have other apps/plans using GTK and they don't want to use two GUI toolkits. KHTML is a light and fast HTML rendering engine, and is based on Trolltech's Qt/Embedded, in most of its incarnations so far.
