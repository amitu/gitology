A friend has been perticularly concerned about Flamingos here in Mumbai:

A group of birdwatchers, including myself have been really concerned about the threat posed to Sewri by the proposed Nhava-Sewri Trans Harbour sealink. Sewri is an area on the eastern waterfront of Mumbai and is home to thousands of wintering birds including flamingoes and shorebirds, which are so numerous as to form clouds over the water, wheeling and turning around.

The bridge is supposed to start off from the Sewri side and will cut right through the bay, ultimately destroying the habitat of these birds. For the last few weeks, a bunch of birdwatchers including myself have begun a campaign to save this site and to demand a re-alignment of the proposed bridge. We feel that the site, which has been declared an IBA (Important Bird Area) under BirdLife International guidelines must be protected for all time...
